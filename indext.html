About Me

I am a Computing Educator and Advocate based in Singapore with over 30 years of experience in transforming Computing and STEM education. My career spans across prestigious institutions including Anderson Serangoon Junior College, National Institute of Education (NIE), Dunman High School, and Victoria Junior College.

My expertise lies in learner-centric pedagogy, adult professional development, and bridging industry-academia gaps. I am nationally recognised for pioneering learner-led initiatives and building vibrant learning communities that foster computational thinking, AI literacy, and innovative computing education approaches.

Adult Educator Attributes, Beliefs & Values

As an adult educator, I embody the core attributes of empathy, cultural responsiveness, and lifelong learning. My belief in the transformative power of education drives my commitment to creating inclusive learning environments. I value diversity, equity, and the unique experiences that each adult learner brings to the educational setting. My approach is grounded in the principle that every adult has the capacity to learn and grow, regardless of their background or previous educational experiences.

Core Educational Philosophy

  • Learner-Centered Approach: Honoring the rich experiences and diverse backgrounds that adult learners bring to educational settings
  • Facilitative Leadership: Guiding and supporting rather than directing, creating environments where learners construct their own knowledge
  • Cultural Responsiveness: Valuing diversity and creating inclusive learning environments that respect different perspectives and learning styles
  • Reflective Practice: Continuous self-assessment and adaptation as fundamental to professional growth and effectiveness
  • Collaborative Learning: Believing in the power of peer learning and community building in educational contexts
